Evaluative Thinking

Evaluative thinking plays an important role in embedding continuous learning and improvement in the Ministry. What is it? Who is it for? Why should I use it?

Video source: Evaluation Capacity Building – Evaluative Thinking by the NSW Government / Duncan Rintoul (external link)
Video transcript: orignal source(external link)

What is Evaluative Thinking?

Evaluative Thinking is about developing a habit of using good evidence to make judgements. It is a way to approach a course of action – a programme, briefing or even just a meeting – in a way that focuses on achieving the outcomes.

Evaluative Thinking involves constantly reflecting and questioning to make informed decisions. It is the state of mind that accompanies continuous improvement.

Who is Evaluation Thinking for?

Evaluative Thinking is for everyone - whether you are an administrator, analyst, designer, manager or executive. The tools and resources available are for anyone in the Ministry of Education to use no matter what their role is.

Why should I use Evaluation Thinking?

Evaluative Thinking helps us be intentional about what we do and to know whether or not we have got to where we wanted to go. It is more than collecting and collating data. It is a disciplined approach to inquiry and reflective practice.

If we improve our capabilities and practices in evaluative thinking then we will be better able to understand the impact of our work and its relationship to improving student outcomes.  We will build a culture of continuous improvement.

Where should I use Evaluation Thinking?

Evaluative Thinking can be applied when we Plan, Do and Review our projects, programme or any initiative.

How should I get started?

The tools below will help you to get started:

Evaluative Thinking Questions

These simple questions are useful questions to ask when you plan, do or review your programme, project or any initiative. You can also ask them when you are developing a policy or even at your team meetings. It is intended to embed evaluative thinking from the get go.

Find out more about the Evaluative Thinking Questions.

 

Logic Model App

The Logic Model web app is a tool that will help you flesh out in detail the short, medium and long term outcomes that your programme, project or initiative intend to deliver. It will also help you visualise how the activities you will deliver will help achieve the outcomes you set out to achieve.

 

Theory of Change

A Theory of Change sets out why a planned activity will lead to an intended result and explain the underlying rationale. It checks on the match between the strategies you are using and the goals you want to achieve. It provides a clear and testable explanation to convincingly show your plan is a really good workable solution.

Read more about the Theory of Change.
