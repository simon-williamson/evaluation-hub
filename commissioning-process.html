Commissioning Process

A new way to start projects, create policy or undertake an evaluation. Commissioning provides clarity at the beginning of a project.

On this page, you will find a template that you can use when you are commissioning a project or being commissioned to undertake a project. Please adapt and use as needed. We have also suggested a simple and pragmatic commissioning process.

Why do commissioning?

Provide greater clarity on what is expected by when

Reduce needless rework

Influence project direction

Recommended commissioning process:

For Policy and Projects

When you and your team have been tasked with developing a policy or starting a new project, the following process should be useful in helping you begin (commissioning).

Step 1

Manager prepares to have the commissioning conversation using the checklist Download [DOCX 860KB]

Step 2

Manager and team member meet to discuss the project

Step 3

Team member records the conversation in the commissioning template and sends it back to the manager to confirm

Step 4

Manager confirms/clarifies commissioning

Step 5

Team member saves the commissioning form in their relevant Filenet folder. Please also send a copy to info@education.govt.nz so we can better understand how it is being used.

Step 6

If changes in scope, priority, happen then the Manager and team member may re-open the commissioning process

For Evaluation

When you and your team are ready to evaluate some past work or current projects, the following process should be helpful in getting started:

Step 1

Decide what the evaluation will be used for

Take the time to consider carefully what is needed from the evaluation, what decisions will be made and when, who it is for and who will benefit from the information
Step 2

Decide the roles and processes in how the evaluation will be undertaken

Decide how decisions will be made on: focus and scope of the evaluation; choosing the evaluator/evaluation team; approving the evaluation plan and funding it; approving evaluation reports and who can access the final report(s) and data.
Step 3

Engage the evaluation team

Evaluations can be conducted by in-house staff (internal) or a third party (external) or a mix of both. Use the Evaluation Commissioning form Download [DOCX 87KB] to help with your initial thinking.
Talk to Analytics and Insights team, EDK for advice on whether an in-house or a third party evaluation or a mix of both is appropriate for you evaluation.
Step 4

Scope the evaluation

Be clear about what is being evaluated. You need to be able to describe what the intervention is and how change will be effected and for whom in practical terms, what does success look like, how the initiative is expected to achieve this and what factors will influence success.
Take the time to consider carefully what the evaluation needs to do before considering possible designs. Ensure all those who need to be consulted during the process are adequately involved.
The RUFDATA [PDF 446KB] categories – Reasons and purpose, Uses, Foci, Data, Audience, Timing and Agency – are useful points to consider when scoping your evaluation.
You can also use the Scoping Evaluation ‘Cheat Sheet’ Download [DOCX 114KB] to gather information for your evaluation plan.
Step 5

Develop the evaluation plan

Develop the formal document that outlines the requirements for the evaluation. This includes the Key Evaluation Questions (KEQs) and the evaluation design – how data will be collected and analysed to answer the KEQs, the implementation plan and a dissemination plan. You can use the Ministry Evaluation Plan template Download [DOCX 79KB]. If you decide to engage an external evaluator, the development of the evaluation plan can be included as a contract deliverable.
Step 6

Manage the implementation of evaluation

How the evaluation will be managed should be agreed in Step 2 and outlined in the implementation plan in Step 4. It is important that the governance group or the management group (depending on what is agreed in Step 2) is informed of the progress of the evaluation and any risks and issues encountered throughout the evaluation.
Step 7

Disseminate reports and support use of the evaluation

Make evaluation reports available and engage with primary intended users to make the results accessible. Use effective strategies to support use of the findings.
Use the Evaluation Report Assessment Tool Download [PDF 369KB] to assess the quality and increase the use of the evaluation findings.
