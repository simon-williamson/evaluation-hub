What is the Evaluation Champions Programme

The Evaluation Champions programme supports embedding a culture of continuous learning across the Ministry. It builds a network of people who are committed to practicing evaluative thinking and it offers training to strengthen these skills. It provides structure and support not only to those who are already doing this in their current roles but also to those who are new to the idea and interested to become an Evaluation Champion.

The programme will establish a community of practice where fellow champions will meet and share their experience tackling culture change, share best practice and support each other.

Who are the Evaluation Champions and what do they do?

The Evaluation Champions are usually promoters and enthusiasts, or drivers and doers of evaluation and evaluative thinking. They feel comfortable with the inquiry process, can support teams with developing Theory of Change, commissioning a policy or evaluation, finding the right resources, know how to access the experts and can facilitate workshops relating to evaluative thinking.

Who can be Evaluation Champions?

No matter what your role, anyone in the Ministry can become an Evaluation Champion.

How do I get started in this role?

  • We will meet with you one-on-one to answer any questions you have and let you know our journey so far
  • We will provide a training for you so you can use the tools and resources available in the Evaluation Hub
  • We will invite you to meet your fellow champions and find out how others are tackling culture change
